      Hiking through Alpstein, Appenzell

      October 2017
      • Distance
      • 20.0 km
      • Ascend:
      • 1468 m
      • Max. altitude:
      • 1810 m
      • Walking time:
      • 6h 03m
      • Days:
      • 2

      Fresh snowfall at the end of October gave this short two-day hike in the Alpstein area a surprisingly Alpine character. After our tour was cut short by a storm the day before, we enjoyed cold but sunny weather and clear skies. We spent a memorable night in the Stauberen mountain inn before continuing to the summit of Hoher Kasten on the next day.

      Those interested in following the tour themselves can find detailed GPS track data here.

      Day 1: From Brülisau to Stauberen
      • Start:

      • End:
      • Stauberen
      • Distance:
      • 14.7 km
      • Walking time:
      • 4h 30m
      • Ascend:
      • 1086 m
      • Max. altitude
      • 1810 m

      Starting our hike in Brülisau, we ascend through Brüeltobel towards the Plattenbödeli inn, where we see first patches of snow. We then continue along the banks of Sämtisersee until we reach Bollenwees near Fälensee. From here, we reach the Saxerlücke and walk the ridge until we arrive at the Stauberen mountain inn, where we spend the night.

      Day 2: From Stauberen to Hoher Kasten
      • Start:
      • Stauberen
      • End:
      • Hoher Kasten
      • Distance:
      • 5.3 km
      • Walking time:
      • 1h 33m
      • Ascend:
      • 382 m
      • Max. altitude:
      • 1777 m

      The second day of our trip starts with a remarkable sunrise, casting breathtaking colours over the Alpstein mountains. We have plenty of time for today's short route, so after enjoying sunrise and breakfast we are up for a late start of our hike. After approximately two hours, we arrive at the summit of Hoher Kasten, where we admire the spectacular view of the Alpstein area and the Sämtisersee below.
